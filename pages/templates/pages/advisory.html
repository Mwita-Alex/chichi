Prof. Chacha Nyaigotti Chacha

Chairperson

Prof. Chacha is a Kenyan playwright and consummate educationalist. Prof. Chacha is currently serving as the Chairman of the Kenyan Commission for University Education (CUE).

Prof. Chacha is a Kenyan playwright and consummate educationalist. Prof. Chacha is currently serving as the Chairman of the Kenyan Commission for University Education (CUE). He has served as an executive secretary of the Inter-University Council for East Africa, first CEO of the Kenyan Higher Education Loans Board (HELB), Pro- Chancellor Mount Kenya University, Vice Chancellor Lukenya University, Principal Kisii University and Laikipia University

He also served as the Chairperson of Kenya Institute of Mass Communication Council. Prof. Chacha was educated at Kenyatta College, receiving a BEd (Swahili Language), and later studied at Yale University, gaining an MA in Anthropological Linguistics and a Ph.D. in Swahili Language Linguistics. He has been a lecturer in the majority of institutions of higher learning in Kenya. He was a Research Professor at the Institute of Regional Integration and Development of the Catholic University of East Africa. Prof. ChachaNyaigotti has shown outstanding performance in all the institutions he has led

Beverly C. Moss

Vice Chairperson

Beverly is the Secretary, Conflict Management and Peace Building Office of the Deputy President. She has worked in various capacities in the private and public sector as well as with civil society organizations for the past twenty - four years

Beverly is the Secretary, Conflict Management and Peace Building Office of the Deputy President. She has worked in various capacities in the private and public sector as well as with civil society organizations for the past twenty - four years. She has also worked as a lecturer at the Nairobi Great Commission School and part time at Daystar University, Department of Peace and International Relations on a voluntary basis and is currently the Vice Chairperson of the Universities and Colleges Students’ Peace Association of Kenya (UCSPAK) Board. In her early years of employment, she worked as an interior designer in various organizations including Victoria Furniture and Hutchings Biemer Ltd.

Beverly holds a Master of Arts Degree in International Conflict Management from the University of Nairobi, a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Peace and Conflict Transformation from Daystar University Nairobi, Kenya and a diploma in Interior Design from Exterior Interior Jenson and Nicholson (India). She is also a Certified Professional Mediator and a member of the Institute of Chartered Mediators and Conciliators. She has undertaken various trainings both locally and internationally including at The United States Institute of Peace (USIP), Washington DC, IPAT (Inter Peace), Switzerland and the Regional Senior Mission Leaders (RSML) course at the International Peace Support Training Centre (IPSTC) Karen.

Dr. Dickson Liyayi Magotsi

Member

Mr. Magotsi is currently serves as the Undersecretary and Head of Programs for the Kenyan Ministry of Interior and Coordination’s National Steering Committee on Peacebuilding

and Conflict Management, housed in the Ministry’s Conflict Early Warning and Response Unit. Mr. Magotsi also sits on the Kenyan National Committee for the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide, War Crimes, Crimes against Humanity and All Forms of Discrimination. During nearly two decades of public service, he has made contributions to a wide variety of peacebuilding and conflict management initiatives, including having served as Team Leader of the UWIANO Platform for Peace. Mr. Magotsi received a B.A in Political Science and an M.A in International Conflict Management from the University of Nairobi. He recently attended the Global Raphael Lemkin Seminar for Genocide Prevention in November of 2017.

Dr. Eliab Some

Member

Dr. Some is an Assistant Professor of Epidemiology and Program Leader for Bachelor of Epidemiology and Biostatistics program, School of Pharmacy and Health Sciences.

He teaches, mentors and coaches bachelors, masters’ and doctoral students on quantitative and qualitative research methods, imparting to the students his practical knowledge acquired from over 30 years of experience in operational research and programme assessment, design, planning, implementation, monitoring, evaluation and review in Kenya, Eastern and Southern Africa, Pacific and Pakistan. Prof. Some has worked with Government of Kenya (6 years), NGOs (13 years), United Nations (WHO & UNICEF) (10 years) and in academia (6 years). Dr. Some has high developed organizational, Emceeing, Rapporteur’s, mobilization and communication competencies

Professor Some’s innovative contribution to public health include: community-based malaria control: promoting the use of insecticide-treated nets and case management; employer-based malaria control: pioneered the first approach in Kenya in the 1990s; faith-based organizations response to HIV/AIDS: pioneered the formulation of policies and strategies in Kenya, Tanzania and Southern Africa; uninterrupted supply of ARV drugs for the management of HIV/AIDS: increased the number of people on ARVs from 2,500 in 2004 to over 315,000 in 2008 in Malawi; uninterrupted supply of vaccines through Pacific Immunizations Strengthening Partnership, covering 22 Pacific Island nations; and pioneering BSc. Epidemiology and Biostatistics at the United St International University – Africa with the first class of 10 students admitted September 2018.
