Brief description about UCSPAK

Universities and Colleges Studentsâ€™ Peace Association of Kenya (UCSPAK) is a non-profit student association whose major objective is to promote peace and good governance in institutions of higher learning in Kenya. UCSPAK was formed after the 2015 Garissa University terror attack. The Association started as the Zero radicalization campaign. Currently UCSPAK has representatives in 69 Universities and Colleges in Kenya representing the interest of over 2 million undergraduate students, mostly age 18-24 years.

Our theory of change emphasizes on youth, especially those in the institutions of higher learning as key agents of change, specifically in the use of non-violent actions to bring about social change. We believe that Students too can be used to build bridges across communities, and work together to mitigate violent conflicts and promote peaceful coexistence.

UCSPAK interventions are focused on five (5) areas namely:
  • (1) Peace Building and Conflict Resolution
  • (2)Leadership and Governance
  • (3)Gender Mainstreaming and Special Needs
  • (4) Youth Economic empowerment
  • (5) Research and Documentation

Vision

The leading youth platform for promoting peace and security in Kenya and beyond.

Mission

To empower and motivate youths to make a positive difference to society through volunteerism, capacity building and tailored mentorship.

Core Values

Volunteerism; Integrity; Excellence; Diversity and Community Partnership
