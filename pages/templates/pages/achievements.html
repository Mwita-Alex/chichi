Electoral Justice Participation

In 2017 UCSPAK Partnered with IEBC and Conducted two separate projects on youth governance and electoral justice. The first project was funded by International Foundation for Electoral Systems- Global Affairs Canada (IFES -GAC) and was titled Increasing Electoral Knowledge Among Young People In The Institutions Of Higher Learning In Kenya (Changamka Project) from June 2017 – November2017. The Changamka project was conducted within institutions of higher learning and surrounding communities in Nairobi, Riftvalley, Coast, Western and Nyanza regions. The project took up the Swahili word “Changamka” which means being vibrant as it encouraged young students to be upbeat about their right to exercise their democratic power through the ballot.Link In the second project, UCSPAK partnered with Saferworld and implemented a project titled ‘Promoting the Role of Youth in Peaceful Elections in Kenya’ Project. This project was Funded by United States - Department of State – Bureau of Democracy, Human Rights and Labor. The project was implemented in Nairobi region in the following institutions- Strathmore University, Technical University of Kenya, Kenyatta University and The University of Nairobi. In both projects, recommendations were that young people are willing to engage in peace processes when accorded the opportunities to do so. Link

The ‘ Amplifying Community voices for Sustainable Peace in Kenya’

From December 2019 to September 2021, UCSPAK implemented a 22 months project titled the ‘ Amplifying Community voices for Sustainable Peace in Kenya’. This project was also known as the People-to-People (P2P) conflict mitigation, Peace building, and reconciliation project. The project was funded by the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) and was implemented in Nairobi, Kisumu, Uasin Gishu, Kiambu and Bungoma counties by a consortium of 5 partners. Two of these organizations were international not-for-profit organizations (Saferworld and Life & Peace Institute) while the rest three were local organizations which included; Universities and Colleges Students’ Peace Association of Kenya, (UCSPAK), Pamoja for Transformation Trust (PfT), and Catholic Justice and Peace Commission (CJPC-Eldoret). Saferworld was the lead organization in this consortium with the other four organizations leading in the implementation of different yet complementary activities. PfT, LPI, and CJPC-Eldoret were implementing community dialogues while UCSPAK was in charge of youth-specific dialogues in all the five targeted counties. Through this project UCSPAK held the first National Student Convention on Youth, Peace and election at the University of Nairobi that was graced by the Cabinet Secretary, Ministry of ICT and Youth Affairs, UN resident Coordinator among other guest. Student-to-student dialogue series to complement the effort of the building bridges to unity advisory task force (BBUAT)

The Student to Student Dialogue Series was conducted by Universities and Colleges Students’ Peace Association of Kenya (UCSPAK) in partnership with Saferworld with the objective of adding the voice of the youth in institutions of learning to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) thus complementing the efforts of the Building Bridges to Unity Advisory Taskforce (BBUAT).The dialogue series were undertaken over a period of two months (July to August, 2019) in five Universities spread across five regions notably; Mount Kenya University (Central region), University of Nairobi (Nairobi region), Moi University (Rift Valley region ), Maseno University (Nyanza region), and the Technical University of Mombasa (Coast region ). These regions were purposively selected based on historical experiences of hotly contested elections in Kenya.

Institutions of higher learning were similarly selected purposively with due consideration of; centrality within the respective region, the size and the heterogeneity of the student population and the level of student involvement in politics as observed in previous elections and the alumni in the national politics. In addition to this, one inter-university dialogue was held in USIU-Africa to consolidate and validate identified issues of concern before submission to the Building Bridges to Unity Advisory Taskforce (BBUAT) in Kenyatta International Convention Center (KICC) on 9th August, 2019. This program was funded by the Embassy of Finland,Nairobi.

The Jisort Youth Economic Empowerment Project

: From January, 2021 to December, 2023 UCSPAK will be implementing a youth economic empowerment project in institutions of higher learning in 5 counties- Nairobi, Kiambu, Nakuru, Kisumu and Mombasa counties- This counties were chosen because they represent counties with most university, college and TVET in Kenya. In total the five counties mentioned above have 241 institutions of higher learning. Nairobi County is leading with 149 institutions, followed by Kiambu County with 26 institutions, Kisumu County follows in the third position with 23 institutions and then Mombasa County with 22 institutions and lastly, Nakuru County with a presence of 21 institutions of higher learning. At the end of the project we aim at empowering over 6,000 young men and women by promoting the savings culture and a mindset change towards entrepreneurship, leveraging on technology for capacity building and advocacy for conducive business environment for young people. This project is funded by Diakonia
